<p><p>The prenatal screening market encompasses several types of tests aimed at assessing the health of the fetus. First-trimester screening tests typically evaluate the risk of chromosomal abnormalities through maternal blood tests and ultrasound. Second-trimester screening expands this assessment, often including additional blood markers and advanced imaging to identify conditions like neural tube defects. Third-trimester screenings focus on monitoring fetal growth and wellbeing. Diagnostic tests, such as amniocentesis and chorionic villus sampling, provide definitive results for suspected abnormalities but carry some risks.</p></p>
