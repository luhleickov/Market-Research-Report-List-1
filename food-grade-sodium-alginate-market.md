<p><p>The food grade sodium alginate market is primarily segmented into two types: powder and granular forms. Powdered sodium alginate is finely milled, offering superior solubility and ease of use in applications like emulsions, gels, and thickening agents. In contrast, granular sodium alginate has a coarser texture, making it ideal for specific applications that require slower dissolution rates. Both forms are essential in food processing, providing stability, texture, and enhancing the quality of various products in the food industry.</p></p>
