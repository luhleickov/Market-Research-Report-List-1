<p><p>The passive shock absorber market comprises three main types: hydraulic, pneumatic, and other types. Hydraulic shock absorbers utilize fluid mechanics to dissipate energy and provide damping, ideal for heavy-duty applications. Pneumatic shock absorbers use air pressure for cushioning, offering lightweight solutions suitable for dynamic environments. Other types may include mechanical or spring-based systems that absorb shock through elastic deformation. Collectively, these technologies enhance safety and comfort in various sectors, including automotive, aerospace, and industrial machinery.</p></p>
